Order of the Bee

We are an independent organization of the Alfresco community. We are here to promote Alfresco Community Edition and aggregate the best from the community for you.

What we do?

Who are we?

We are an organization of Alfresco community members working to grow the Alfresco ecosystem independent of Alfresco Software, Inc.

We are all different: some of us are employees at Alfresco Software, its customers, and partners, working with Alfresco Community Edition in addition to our regular jobs. Others use Alfresco Community edition as end users, implement it for the customers, and develop free and paid add-ons. But we have something in common: we like Alfresco and its community, we think that Alfresco Community edition and open source solutions based on it are very important for us and the whole market.

Our mission is to guarantee the existence of Alfresco Community edition as a free/libre open source solution for document management.

We were worried that the current management of Alfresco didn't see the value of Alfresco being open source, but for us, that's a core driver for this great community. We also think that Alfresco wouldn't be what it is without its community. And by community, we don't just mean users of Alfresco Community edition, but also people using Alfresco Enterprise, Alfresco partners, and Alfresco employees willing to collaborate with each other to create better software, and a better community.

We are working on different tasks to make Alfresco Community more open for you. We will make easier for you to find and to use the best free extensions for Alfresco. We'll manage lists of well-tried systems integrators and freelancers. We aggregate and publish best practices and success stories of users who work with Alfresco Community edition.

What we are doing?

"Showcase" and "Testbed" instances

We are working on building and supporting several servers with Alfresco installed that will be available for the public.

"Showcase" instances are used to show different free Alfresco add-ons.

If someone turns up into the IRC channel with a question we could log him/her onto the testbed instance and demonstrate certain points of configuration. "Testbed" instances will be rebuilt every night and on demand.

We work on making this activity alive. Follow us to be informed when instances will be ready.

Current projects and ideas


Forming a "honeycomb" of free add-ons

Honeycomb is a collection of free open source Alfresco add-ons, that work on the latest releases and look useful and interesting for many people in the community.

The Alfresco add-ons site is a bit of a mess. Some addons are obsolete, some are already included to Alfresco core but are still listed, many are proprietary and none of them are categorised.

While forming the collection we are playing the following roles:

  • reviewing that an add-on works on the latest Alfresco versions
  • categorizing add-ons
  • collaborating with add-on's creator on arisen problems
  • creating and reviewing a summary of add-on, tutorials and screenshots

Go to Honeycomb


Forming a list of System Integrators and Freelancers

Many companies search for a team or a person who can implement Alfresco Community edition, help to develop an extension, or solve some specific task. As far as Alfresco Inc. forbids partners to support Alfresco Community edition, it is difficult to find an experienced reliable team or person for such task.

We form a list of well-tried systems integrators and freelancers who can help you to solve technical issues all over the world. The complexity is that we cannot verify the work of all teams and people. So the list will be rather empty in the beginning. You may help us to form the list by following actions:

  • If you provide services: send us a link to your profile, tell us what you can do and give us contacts of one or several customers whom you already helped.
  • If you are a customer: share with us your story, tell us who helped you to implement Alfresco Community edition in your company and was is good or bad.

Alfresco OOTB Edition

A bit ambitious, this would be kind of a combination of some of the ideas, involving creating scripts, content models, workflows and patches to build our own community edition, perhaps this would be what the showcase instance was built from. It's ambitious because it would need a lot of testing and would probably need to be rebuilt and tested with every 'letter' release of CE (e.g. 4.2.g)

Currently we don't work on this idea, but we will start doing it as far as we start other (easier) activities. It does have a kind of branding advantage for the OOTB group but if it detracts from the current community offering it might be disadvantageous.

We are going to start working on this activity. Follow us to learn more about the current stage.
