Who are we?

We are an organization of Alfresco community members working to grow the Alfresco ecosystem independent of Alfresco Software, Inc.

We are all different: some of us are paying customers; some work for partners; and some are Alfresco Software employees. Some of us work with Alfresco Community Edition while others work with Alfresco Enterprise Edition, or both. Other members use Alfresco as end users, implement it for customers, and develop free and paid add-ons. But we have something in common: we like Alfresco and its community and we think that Alfresco Community Edition and open source solutions based on it are very important to the whole market.

Our mission is to guarantee the existence of Alfresco Community Edition as a free/libre open source solution for document management.

Top Activities


Honeycomb of the greatest addons

We maintain a listing of high-value, high-quality addons within the Alfresco ecosystem. The add-ons that are included work on the latest releases and are those that many people find useful and interesting. We're picky about which add-ons we choose because we want to be able to help you use them on your projects.


Honeycomb

Professional Services Network

Many companies are looking for a team or a person who can implement a project based on Alfresco Community Edition. Or perhaps they need help to develop an extension or solve some specific task. Alfresco Software forbids partners to do paid work on Alfresco Community Edition, so it can be difficult to find an experienced, reliable team to help with Community Edition.

To address this, we are forming a list of well-known systems integrators and freelancers all over the world who can help you with Community Edition.


Professional Services Network

Tutorials, Articles and Blog Posts

We monitor technical Alfresco-related blogs every day and add the most helpful tutorials, articles and posts to our archive.

So if you do not know how to use the Alfresco Maven SDK or how to create custom workflows, welcome to our archive. Here you can find new features and add-ons, small hacks and tips that can be useful during Alfresco implementation, and stories that our members want to share with the community.
