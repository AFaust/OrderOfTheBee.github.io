Order of the Bee

We are an independent organization of the Alfresco community. We are here to promote Alfresco Community Edition and aggregate the best from the community for you.

What we do?

Who are we?

We are an organization of Alfresco community members working to grow the Alfresco ecosystem independent of Alfresco Software, Inc.

We are all different: some of us are employees at Alfresco Software, its customers, and partners. Some of us work with Alfresco Community Edition, others with Alfresco Enterprise Edition, or both. Other members use Alfresco as end users, implement it for the customers, and develop free and paid add-ons. But we have something in common: we like Alfresco and its community, we think that Alfresco Community edition and open source solutions based on it are very important for us and for the whole market.

Our mission is to guarantee the existence of Alfresco Community edition as a free/libre open source solution for document management.

Current projects and ideas



Forming a "honeycomb" of free add-ons

Honeycomb is a collection of our favorite free open source Alfresco add-ons, that work on the latest releases and are useful and interesting for many people in the community. We choose only add-ons that we use or develop ourselves, or collaborate with their developers tightly. So we can guarantee that at least most of the announced features work and help users with arisen problems.

While forming the collection we are playing the following roles:

  • reviewing that an add-on works on the latest Alfresco versions
  • categorizing add-ons
  • collaborating with add-on's creator on arisen problems
  • creating and reviewing tutorials and screenshots

Full collection of add-ons can be found at Alfresco add-ons site.

Forming a list of System Integrators and Freelancers

Many companies search for a team or a person who can implement a project based on Alfresco Community edition in their companies, help to develop an extension, or solve some specific task. As far as Alfresco Inc. forbids partners to support Alfresco Community edition, it is difficult to find an experienced reliable team or person for such task.

We form a list of well-tried systems integrators and freelancers who can help you to solve technical issues all over the world. We verify the work of all teams and people we publish, so the list is not very long, but we are sure, that we recommend you a proven team, and we are working on make the list longer.

You may help us to form the list by following actions:

  • If you provide services: send us a link to your profile, tell us what you can do and give us contacts of one or several customers whom you already helped.
  • If you are a customer: share with us your story, tell us who helped you to implement Alfresco Community edition in your company and was is good or bad.

Alfresco Live In development

We are working on building and supporting several servers with Alfresco installed that will be available for the public.

  • The first one is a Showcase instance, that is used to show different free Alfresco add-ons. After we will configure it you may just login and meet add-ons from our Honeycomb.
  • The second one is a Testbed server. If someone turns up into the IRC channel with a question we could log him/her onto the testbed instance and demonstrate certain points of configuration. Join #alfresco in IRC to ask us for a help.

All servers will be rebuilt every night and on demand.

We work on making this activity alive. Follow us to be informed when instances will be ready.

Alfresco OOTB Edition In development

We are working on creating Alfresco OOTB Edition with blackjack and hookers. It will be based on Alfresco Community Edition and include a combination of scripts, content models, workflows, extensions and patches.

As far as Alfresco is very Enterprise, it's very difficult to send them a patch and to get it approved. Even if it is approved, you can wait for a release with your patch inside for a several years. So we decided to start working on custom edition to include some of the greatest add-ons inside and build a number of scripts to install Alfresco quicker on our Alfresco Live instances.

We are not going to fork Alfresco to build our own product. The idea is to build a new package that will include unmodified Alfresco distribution and a number of scripts that will apply our changes to the system during the first start.

We are going to start working on this activity. Follow us to learn more about the current stage.
