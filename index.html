Order of the Bee

We are an independent organization of the Alfresco community. We are here to promote Alfresco Community Edition and aggregate the best from the community for you.

What we do?

Who are we?

We are an organization of Alfresco community members working to grow the Alfresco ecosystem independent of Alfresco Software, Inc.

We are all different: some of us are paying customers; some work for partners; and some are Alfresco Software employees. Some of us work with Alfresco Community Edition while others work with Alfresco Enterprise Edition, or both. Other members use Alfresco as end users, implement it for the customers, and develop free and paid add-ons. But we have something in common: we like Alfresco and its community and we think that Alfresco Community Edition and open source solutions based on it are very important to the whole market.

Our mission is to guarantee the existence of Alfresco Community Edition as a free/libre open source solution for document management.

Join the Order List all members




We welcome all community members who want to contribute to the Alfresco community. There are a lot of ways to contribute and here are just a few:

  • Publishing tutorials and posts about using, configuring and developing on Alfresco
  • Hosting and organizing Alfresco Meetups
  • Helping people on Forums, in IRC, StackOverflow and other resources
  • Developing free open-source add-ons
  • Translating Alfresco and add-ons to your native language
  • And many other ways when you share with others something helpful with no charge.

The only requirement and the only way to join the Order is to contribute to the community. Please, tell us about yourself and about your contributions so we may know a little about you. Links and details are welcome.

Current projects and ideas



Forming a "honeycomb" of free add-ons

Honeycomb is a collection of our favorite free open source Alfresco add-ons. The add-ons that are included work on the latest releases and are those that many people find useful and interesting. We're picky about which add-ons we choose because we want to be able to help you use them on your projects.

While forming the collection we are playing the following roles:

  • reviewing that an add-on works on the latest Alfresco versions
  • categorizing add-ons
  • collaborating with the add-on's developer when problems arise
  • creating and reviewing tutorials and screenshots

The full collection of add-ons can be found on the Alfresco Add-Ons site.

Go to Honeycomb



Forming a list of System Integrators and Freelancers

Many companies search for a team or a person who can implement a project based on Alfresco Community Edition. Or perhaps they need help to develop an extension or to solve some specific task. Alfresco Software forbids partners to do paid work on Alfresco Community Edition, so it can be difficult to find an experienced, reliable team to help with Community Edition.

So we are forming a list of well-known systems integrators and freelancers all over the world who can help you with Community Edition.

Want to help us form the list? Here's how:

  • If you provide services: send us a link to your profile, tell us what you can do, and give us contacts of one or more customers who are willing to be a reference.
  • If you are a customer: share your story and tell us who helped you with your Alfresco Community Edition project.

Forming a list of Success Stories

Many companies use Alfresco Enterprise and Alfresco Software does a good job of telling their stories. But many organizations, large and small, use Alfresco Community Edition and they are happy with it. One of our goals is to tell their story.

We want you to share your Alfresco Community Edition story with the rest of the world. What issues arose and how did you address them? Who helped you in hard times? What do you wish you had known when you first started out? Contact us using the Mailing list.

Alfresco Live In development

We are working on building and supporting several servers with Alfresco installed that will be available for the public.

  • The first one is a Showcase instance, that is used to show different free Alfresco add-ons. After we configure it you can login and explore add-ons from our Honeycomb.
  • The second one is a Testbed server. If someone shows up in the IRC channel with a question we can log them into the testbed instance and answer the question hands-on. Join #alfresco in IRC to ask us for a help.

All servers will be rebuilt every night and on demand.

We are working on this currently. Follow us to be informed when instances will be ready.

Alfresco OOTB Edition In development

We are working on creating Alfresco OOTB (Order of the Bee) Edition. It will be based on Alfresco Community Edition and will include a combination of scripts, content models, workflows, extensions and patches.

Alfresco OOTB Edition will include some of the most popular and useful add-ons that are available as well as a number of scripts that can be used to install Alfresco more quickly.

We are not going to fork Alfresco to build our own product. The idea is to build a new package that will include the unmodified Alfresco Community Edition distribution and a number of scripts that will apply our changes to the system during the first start.

We are working on this currently. Follow us to learn more about the current stage.
