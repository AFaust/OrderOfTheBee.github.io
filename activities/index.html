Current projects and ideas


Forming a list of the best free add-ons

We maintain a list of our favorite free open source Alfresco add-ons. The add-ons that are included work on the latest releases and are those that many people find useful and interesting. We're picky about which add-ons we choose because we want to be able to help you use them on your projects.

Maintaining the list involves:

  • reviewing that an add-on works on the latest Alfresco versions
  • categorizing add-ons
  • collaborating with the add-on's developer when problems arise
  • creating and reviewing tutorials and screenshots

The homepage of the ADDONS committee and all details on how to add your add-on to the list can be found at Github Wiki.

Business


Forming a Professional Services Network

Many companies are looking for a team or a person who can implement a project based on Alfresco Community Edition. Or perhaps they need help to develop an extension or solve some specific task. Alfresco Software forbids partners to do paid work on Alfresco Community Edition, so it can be difficult to find an experienced, reliable team to help with Community Edition.

To address this, we are forming a list of well-known systems integrators and freelancers all over the world who can help you with Community Edition.

Want to help us form the list? Here's how:

  • If you provide services: send us a link to your profile, tell us what you can do, and give us contacts of one or more customers who are willing to be a reference.
  • If you are a customer: share your story and tell us who helped you with your Alfresco Community Edition project.

Honeycomb Development In development


Alfresco Honeycomb Edition

We are working on creating Alfresco Honeycomb Edition. It will be based on Alfresco Community Edition and will include a combination of scripts, content models, workflows, extensions and patches.

Alfresco Honeycomb Edition will include some of the most popular and useful add-ons that are available as well as a number of scripts that can be used to install Alfresco more quickly.

We are not going to fork Alfresco to build our own product. The idea is to build a new package that will include the unmodified Alfresco Community Edition distribution and a number of scripts that will apply our changes to the system during the first start.

We are working on this currently. Follow us to learn more about the current stage.

Alfresco Live

We are working on building and supporting several servers with Alfresco installed that will be available for the public.

  • The first one is a Showcase instance, that is used to show different free Alfresco add-ons. After we configure it you can login and explore add-ons from our Honeycomb.
  • The second one is a Testbed server. If someone shows up in the IRC channel with a question we can log them into the testbed instance and answer the question hands-on. Join #alfresco in IRC to ask us for a help.

All servers will be rebuilt every night and on demand.

We are working on this currently. Follow us to be informed when instances will be ready.

Upcoming events


Hack-a-thon

Hack-a-thon is a one-day event to work on some Alfresco add-ons in teams or individually.

Alfresco provides two hack-a-thon events per year:

  • Global Virtual Hack-a-thons (Each spring)
  • Alfresco Summit Hack-a-thons (Each fall, the day before Alfresco Summit)

We currently participate in these events, organize local hack-a-thons in different countries and gather together to participate in Global Virtual Hack-a-thons. Submit your project idea in the "Collaboration on building add-ons" activity or just join the Hack-a-thon.

Next hack-a-thon: Alfresco Virtual Hack-a-thon 2015 (May 2015)

Tech Talk Live

Tech Talk Live is a series of online regularly scheduled 1-hour sessions where attendees discuss technical questions or present new Alfresco features, development tools or free open-source add-ons.

Alfresco hosts Tech Talks since 2008. Order of the Bee is starting to provide our own Tech Talk sessions to make them happen more often and to discuss the Honeycomb development process.

Submit a request to add your topic to the schedule or tell us what topics are interesting for you.

Localization


Alfresco and add-ons localization

Alfresco is not translated to your language? Want your customers to use their favorite add-on in their native language? Join our project to localize Alfresco related products.

  • More than 400 people already work on translating Alfresco to their languages. We help them to find other collaborators and to package their language packs to make it easier to install them to Alfresco. Join an existing team or create your own team.
  • Want to translate an add-on to your language? We collaborate with many developers to make their add-ons translatable and to start translation projects for their add-ons.
